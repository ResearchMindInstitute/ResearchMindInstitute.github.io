Research Mind Book Club

The Research Mind Book Club's purpose is to create a passage of maturity in the reading journeys of students, driving participants to read challenging but beneficial books and reflect on them through student discussion. Inspired by the idea extended by How to Read a Book, group discussions motivate students to read nonfiction books for purposes of personal development over entertainment, suggesting books selected to increase quantity and quality, depth and breadth, and thought and action behind the reader's intellectual journey. Members will cultivate reading, thinking, and analysis skills by discussing passages and answering far-reaching questions in club meetings and discussions. Moreover, they will be made to consider ways to apply the acquired information to their life, achieving insight, resolution, and understanding from the text.


Reading and Thinking Guidance for 02/23/26 Meeting

Chapters 2 & 3 of Hearts Touched with Fire

  1. Suggested keywords: crucibles, transformation,
  2. What kind of transformation did Franklin Delano Roosevelt undergo? What did he try to show to the people, and why do you think so? What does this reveal about his character?
  3. What exactly distinguishes certain leaders in their reactions to various "crucibles"? How might this quality be developed?
Although discussions will not be limited to these topics, we encourage you to read with these questions in mind, as to give a purpose to read and a topic to hone your "brain muscles" with!
